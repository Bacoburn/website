Chicago Hyperloop

Bringing Better Futures faster


We are a UIC student lead organization who build and race hyperloop pods to help the world switch to a more eco-friendly, faster, and reliable mode of transportation.

A Hyperloop, is an electric transportation method, that uses electromagnets to levitate above an aluminum track. The track is placed in a vacuum tube to reduce friction, increasing its efficiency and maximum velocity. The Hyperloop is made in small pods to ensure efficiency and make it a more on demand service.

What we do

Our organization is broken down into several subteams and projects, our LIM team focuses on building the linear induction motor, a 3 phase motor that allows us to levitate and propel our pod. Our Structures team focuses on structures and mechanical systems, from carbon fiber aerodynamic shells to suspension and hydraulic systems. Our Controls team focuses on electrical and software mechanisms, from safety sensors in our arduino's and raspberry pi's, to remote control. Our Marketing and design team focuses on maintaining our website, photography, and sponsorships. Breaking our organization into this many subteams allows us to keep our schedule flexible, and maintain social distancing in our small space.

We do everything from CAD, circuit design, and software engineering to help prototype our pods before competition. We built a pod in the past to compete in the SpaceX hyperloop competition, but due to covid that event was canceled. Now we focus on building a pod for the European Hyperloop Week in July 2023.
